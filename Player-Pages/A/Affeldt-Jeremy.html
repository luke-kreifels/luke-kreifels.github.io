Career with the Royals

Jeremy Affeldt started his 14 year career on the Royals pitching in 184 games across 5 season before being sent at the 2006 trade deadline to the Rockies. He was a reliable presence on the 2000's Royals pitching staff providing almost 400 innings of work which included starts, bullpen appearances and closing out games.

After the Royals he went on to play for the Rockies, Reds, and Giants, where he was apart of 3 World Series winning teams including the 2014 team that beat the Royals for the championship. He finished his career with a 3.97 ERA across 926 innings and had a insane 0.86 ERA across 31.1 postseason innings. His postseason excellence also includes a 10-1 team record in postseason series with only 1 run given up in 11.2 innings and 4 World Series appearances.
