Career with the Royals

Willie Aikens joined the Royals via an offseason trade and provided four productive seasons, consistently ranking among the team's statistical leaders. His best season came in 1980 when he hit 20 home runs and drove in 98 runs.

Aikens also excelled in the postseason, batting .375 across 12 playoff games. In the 1980 World Series, he was a standout performer, hitting four home runs, driving in eight runs, and posting a .400 average.

He was a the 2nd overall pick in the 1975 draft and also played for the Angels and Blue Jays before playing professionally in Mexico.
